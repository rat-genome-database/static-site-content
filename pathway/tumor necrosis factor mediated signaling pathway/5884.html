Notes Activation of IKK by TNF-alpha requires site-specific ubiquitination of RIP1. Activation of IKK by TNF-alpha requires site-specific ubiquitination of RIP1. FAK overexpression in human tumors provides a survival signal function by binding to RIP and inhibiting its interaction with the death receptor complex. RIP is one of the critical components involved in mediating DNA damage-induced, p53-independent cell death. RIP may serve as a general activator of IRF7, responding to and transmitting the signals from various stimuli, and that ubiquitination may be a general mechanism for enhancing the activity of IRF7. TAK1 is recruited to the TNF-R1 complex via RIP and likely cooperates with MEKK3 to activate NF-kappaB in TNF-alpha signaling. TNF-induced TRAF2-RIP1-AIP1-ASK1 complex formation and for the activation of ASK1-JNK/p38 apoptotic signaling. The expression of receptor-interacting protein 1 (RIP1) in peripheral blood mononuclear cells was significantly decreased in SLE patients. This study provides the first genetic evidence that the ubiquitination of RIP is required for TNFalpha-induced NF-kappaB activation. We describe how RIP1 acts as a key integrator of signalling pathways initiated by stimulation of death receptors, bacterial or viral infection, genotoxic stress and T-cell homeostasis. Cells pre-stimulated through TNFR-2 prior to subsequent activation of TNFR-1, showed enhanced cell death and recruitment of RIP to the TNFR-1 complex; deficiency of RIP rescued the infected cells from TNF-induced cytotoxicity. Findings show ubiquitination of RIP1 inhibits TNF-induced apoptosis first through an NF-kappaB-independent mechanism, then through an NF-kappaB-dependent mechanism; in absence of ubiquitination, RIP1 serves as a proapoptotic signaling molecule.
