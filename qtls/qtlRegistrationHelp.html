QTL Symbol:

Enter the preferred QTL symbol root. In general, the symbol root will consist of the initial letters of each word of the QTL root name. This should begin with an upper case letter and consist of only letters and Arabic numeral.

 

QTL Name:

A QTL name consists of:

  • a root name describing the measured trait
  • the designation QTL (recommended)
  • a serial number

Enter the preferred QTL root name. Choose from the list provided or enter another name if your QTL cannot be described by any of the traits listed. RGD will provide the next serial number in the serires and inform the submitter.

 

Trait:

The trait is the phenotype measured. This should reflect the actual phenotype and not the disease. For example, for a rat model of arthritis, the trait ma be joint bone inflammation.

 

Subtrait:

The subtrait is further refinement of the trait measured.

 

Trait Description:

The trait description describes how the trait was measured, for example:

  • oral glucose tolerance test after overnight starvation

 

Strains Crossed:

Enter the strains used in determining this QTL. If the strain is not listed, please register the strain using the strain registration form. If the contact information is the same, you need only enter the required information.

 

Chromosome:

Enter the chromosome on which the QTL is located.

 

LOD Score:

Enter the LOD at the peak of the QTL.

 

Flanking Markers 1 and 2:

Enter the nearest genetic markers at the extremes of the QTL region.

 

Candidate Genes:

List any genes that may be responsible for the phenotype measured.

 

Reference:

List the PubMed IDs of publication(s) describing this gene. If a PubMed ID is not available, enter the citation (author, title, journal, volume, page number).

 

Comments:

Enter any other information of questions, etc.
