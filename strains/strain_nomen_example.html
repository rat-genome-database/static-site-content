Condensed Strain Nomenclature Guidelines
Strain TypeCharacteristicsNomenclature
Inbred StrainMated b x s for 20+ consecutive generationsParent and offspring mating Symbol designated by capital letter or letters (four letters or fewer) in Roman type eg. BN, SHR
Related Inbred StrainStrain with common origin but separated before F20Symbol should indicate the relationshipSymbols should be together in the alphabetical list (e.g. SS SR)
SubstrainSeparated after 20 but before 40 generations of b x s mating Genetic differences (residual heterozygosity or mutation) from other branches are discoveredBranch is separated for 20+ generationName of the parent strain slash ILAR code (if a lab has more than one substrain serial number is added before the ILAR code) eg. BN/NHsdMcwi
Recombinant Inbred StrainFormed by crossing 2 inbred strains followed by 20+ generations of b x s mating Abbreviation of both parental strain names separated with capital X with no spaceDifferent RI strains in a series is distinguished by numbers following the symbol BXH1, BXH2
Coisogenic StrainInbred strains that differ at only a single locus through mutation occurring in that strainStrain symbol or substrain symbol followed by a hyphen and the gene symbol of the differential allele in italics eg XX-<I>Abc</I> which will be displayed as XX-Abc
Congenic StrainProduced by repeated backcrosses to inbred (background) strain with selection for a particular marker from the donor strain.Minimum 10 backcross generation to the background strain should be madeAbbreviated symbol of the recipient strain separated by period from abbreviated symbol of the donor strain hyphen the differential allele(s) If a chromosomal segment has been transferred list the most proximal and the most distal markers in parenthesis separated by a hyphen slash ILAR code of the lab eg. SHR.BN-(<i>D13Arb5-Ren</i>)/Ipcv will be displayed as SHR.BN-(D13Arb5-Ren)/Ipcv.
Consomic Strain/Chromosome substitution strainProduced by repeated backcrossing of the whole chromosome onto an inbred strainMinimum of 10 backcross generations is requiredAAbbreviated host strain hyphen chromosome number strain of origin is all capitalized superscript eg SHR.Y<sup>BN</sup> will be displayed asSHR.YBN
Segregating Inbred StrainsInbred strains in which a particular allele or mutation is maintained in heterozygous stateNamed as inbred strains and the segregated locus is a part of the standard genotype
Conplastic Strain Nuclear genome from one strain has been crossed onto the cytoplasm of another, mitochondrial donor is always the female parentAbbreviated strain with the nuclear genome hyphen mt (mitochondrial) superscript strain of the cytoplasmic genome

P.S. Please refer to 'The Rules for Nomenclature of Mouse and Rat Strains'
(http://www.rgd.mcw.edu/nomen/rules-for-nomen.shtml) for details
