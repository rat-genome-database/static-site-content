Definition of Rat Community Forum

"The Rat Community Forum (RCF) is an online bulletin board which is intended to be used by any member of the rat community to ask and answer rat-related questions."

The Rat Community Forum is also known as “RCF”.

What is RCF?

The Rat Community Forum (RCF) is hosted by The Rat Genome Database. Users can access RCF at http://rgd.mcw.edu/rcf or by visiting RGD’s homepage and clicking on “Rat Forum”, under the “Community” header.

RCF is a moderated forum, where all posts are reviewed for content before posting. RCF has five forums, each of which is intended for a particular type of question or announcement:

  • General Questions and Help
  • Nomenclature
  • News
  • Meetings and Workshops
  • Employment and Opportunities

Users must register with RCF in order to be able to post to the forum. However, you need not be a registered user to view posts at : http://rgd.mcw.edu/RCF/

Within each forum, you are able to view posts regarding the specific topic and post to that forum. The forum makes it easy to view and reply to postings by other users, the postings are also searchable making it a growing resource of rat-related questions and answers

RCF was amalgamated with the MGI Rat List in 2002; the archives of the old RCF and Rat List postings are available.

Registering as a User of RCF

To register as a user of The Rat Community Forum, please follow these steps.

  • Open web browser to: http://rgd.mcw.edu/RCF/
  • Double click “Login”
  • Double click: “Click here to register and create an account”
  • Fill out the registration form and submit
  • A temporary password will then be sent to your registered email address.
  • Obtain password from email.
  • Return to: http://rgd.mcw.edu/RCF/
  • Double Click “Login”
  • Enter your username and password and click “Login”
  • Double Click “User”
  • Choose “Change Password”
  • Type in temporary (old) password
  • Type in a new password that you will remember and submit.
Now you are a registered user of The Rat Community Forum with a username and password created by you.

Posting to RCF

In order to post to RCF, you must be a registered user. For details on registering, please see RCF help topic, “Registering as a user of RCF”.

Registered users can properly post messages in the following manner:

1. Open the link the RCF site: http://rgd.mcw.edu/RCF/

2. Login

3. Click on the forum upon which you would like to submit a message (i.e. "Meetings and Workshops")

4. Click on "Post" within the chosen forum

5. Post you Title and Message within the given area.

6. You can then preview your message and post to the RCF site.

Internally your message is received at RGD, but your message will not post automatically. It is reviewed for content and proper formatting manually and then will be released to the RCF site.

Viewing Posts

If you are a registered user of RCF you will get automatically receive and email when a new message is posted to the forum. Or you may view all messages posted to RCF in a given forum by visiting: http://rgd.mcw.edu/RCF/. You need not be a member of RCF to view postings via the RCF website.

The forum makes it easy to view postings and related replies by aligning original posts and their related messages together.

Replying to Post

You may reply to RCF posts in two different ways. You can reply to an RCF message you received via email or reply via the RCF website. Please note replies sent to either a RCF post via email or website, is sent through the RCF system , messages are not personal replies to the send only.

Replying via email message:

  1. Click “Reply” to the RCF posting that was sent to you via email.
  2. This response message will then be sent to the RCF system for review and posting.

Replying via RCF website:

  1. Open: http://rgd.mcw.edu/rcf
  2. Login using your user name and password
  3. Open the forum where the original post is located
  4. Open the original message
  5. Click the reply (see example below)
  6. Post your response in the message box.
  7. Click “Preview” to ensure your message displays correctly.
  8. Once your message appears correctly, click “Post”
