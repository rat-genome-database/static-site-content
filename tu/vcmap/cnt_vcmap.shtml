RGD Definition

Virtual Comparative Map (VCMap) is a computational tool that facilitates in silico prediction of syntenic regions between rat, mouse and human. It utilizes the available map information for markers and ESTs (Unigene) from all three species. The output of this comparison is a comparative map providing a visual representation of the chromosome of the primary (backbone) species plus syntenic regions from the two other species. The maps are clickable allowing you to view further information on the mapped markers and clusters plus bin reports showing the build data for a defined chromosomal region.

Mapping data used in the current VCMap build (v5, July 2002)

  • Rat: RH map v2.1, Rat Genome Database, http://rgd.mcw.edu
  • Mouse: Mouse RH map Release 9, Whitehead Institute; http://www-genome.wi.mit.edu/mouse_rh/
  • Human: GB4 RH maps (GeneMap99, http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/genemap99/)

Finding Gene and Marker information using VCMap

There are two ways to search the VCMap data:

  1. By querying the VCMap data for a particular marker (query by gene, unigene or accession number)
  2. By displaying a particular region of the maps (query by species/location)

To obtain more information, click on Search VCMap from the Detailed Help and follow the instructions.

Related Information/Links

All the microsatellite markers on a VCMap prediction output are hyperlinked to their reports in RGD. All the Unigenes and known genes from different reports are hyperlinked to NCBI Unigene or GenBank respectively.

The VCMap Reports

Apart from producing visual representation of the syntenic regions from the three species, VCMap also generates individual reports for markers, chromosomal regions, map data bin predictions and stream predictions. Descriptions of these reports can be obtained from the following detailed VCMap help pages [ More... ].
